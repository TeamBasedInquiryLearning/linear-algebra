Task 4.2.6.a.
To find \(\vec x = T^{-1}(\vec{e}_1)\text{,}\) we need to find the unique solution for \(T(\vec x)=\vec e_1\text{.}\) Which of these linear systems can be used to find this solution?
  1. \(\displaystyle \begin{array}{cccc} 2x_1 & -1x_2 & -6x_3 & =x_1 \\ 2x_1 & +1x_2 & +3x_3 & =0 \\ 1x_1 & +1x_2 & +4x_3 & =0 \end{array}\)
  2. \(\displaystyle \begin{array}{cccc} 2x_1 & -1x_2 & -6x_3 & =x_1 \\ 2x_1 & +1x_2 & +3x_3 & =x_2 \\ 1x_1 & +1x_2 & +4x_3 & =x_3 \end{array}\)
  3. \(\displaystyle \begin{array}{cccc} 2x_1 & -1x_2 & -6x_3 & =1 \\ 2x_1 & +1x_2 & +3x_3 & =0 \\ 1x_1 & +1x_2 & +4x_3 & =0 \end{array}\)
  4. \(\displaystyle \begin{array}{cccc} 2x_1 & -1x_2 & -6x_3 & =1 \\ 2x_1 & +1x_2 & +3x_3 & =1 \\ 1x_1 & +1x_2 & +4x_3 & =1 \end{array}\)
