Task 2.3.9.b.
Which of these statements is valid?
  1. \(\left[\begin{array}{c} 1 \\ 1 \\ 1 \end{array}\right]\in W\text{,}\) and \(\left[\begin{array}{c} 2 \\ 2 \\ 2 \end{array}\right]\in W\text{,}\) so \(W\) is a subspace.
  2. \(\left[\begin{array}{c} 1 \\ 1 \\ 1 \end{array}\right]\in W\text{,}\) and \(\left[\begin{array}{c} 2 \\ 2 \\ 2 \end{array}\right]\in W\text{,}\) so \(W\) is not a subspace.
  3. \(\left[\begin{array}{c} 1 \\ 1 \\ 1 \end{array}\right]\in W\text{,}\) but \(\left[\begin{array}{c} 2 \\ 2 \\ 2 \end{array}\right]\not\in W\text{,}\) so \(W\) is a subspace.
  4. \(\left[\begin{array}{c} 1 \\ 1 \\ 1 \end{array}\right]\in W\text{,}\) but \(\left[\begin{array}{c} 2 \\ 2 \\ 2 \end{array}\right]\not\in W\text{,}\) so \(W\) is not a subspace.
