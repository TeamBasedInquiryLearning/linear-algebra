Activity 3.4.13.

Let \(T: \IR^n \rightarrow \IR^m\) be a linear map with standard matrix \(A\text{.}\) Determine whether each of the following statements means \(T\) is (A) injective, (B) surjective, or (C) bijective (both).
  1. The kernel of \(T\) is trivial, i.e. \(\ker T=\{\vec 0\}\text{.}\)
  2. The image of \(T\) equals its codomain, i.e. \(\Im T=\IR^m\text{.}\)
  3. For every \(\vec w\in \IR^m\text{,}\) the set \(\{\vec v\in \IR^n|T(\vec v)=\vec w\}\) contains exactly one vector.
