Activity 2.5.6.

The vectors
\begin{align*} \hat\imath &= (1,0,0)=\left[\begin{array}{c}1 \\ 0 \\ 0 \\ \end{array}\right] & \hat\jmath &= (0,1,0)=\left[\begin{array}{c}0 \\ 1 \\ 0 \end{array}\right] & \hat k &=(0,0,1)= \left[\begin{array}{c}0 \\ 0 \\ 1 \end{array}\right] \end{align*}
form a basis \(\{\hat\imath,\hat\jmath,\hat k\}\) used frequently in multivariable calculus.
Find the unique linear combination of these vectors
\begin{equation*} \unknown\hat\imath+\unknown\hat\jmath+\unknown\hat k \end{equation*}
that equals the vector
\begin{equation*} (3,-2,4)=\left[\begin{array}{c}3 \\ -2 \\ 4\end{array}\right] \end{equation*}
in \(xyz\) space.
