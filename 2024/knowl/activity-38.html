Activity 2.3.4.

Consider the homogeneous vector equation \(x_1 \vec{v}_1 + \cdots+x_n \vec{v}_n = \vec{0}\text{.}\)
(a)
Is this equation consistent?
  1. no.
  2. yes.
  3. more information is required.
(b)
Note that if \(\left[\begin{array}{c} a_1 \\ \vdots \\ a_n \end{array}\right] \) and \(\left[\begin{array}{c} b_1 \\ \vdots \\ b_n \end{array}\right] \) are both solutions, we know that
\begin{equation*} a_1 \vec{v}_1+\cdots+a_n \vec{v}_n = \vec{0} \text{ and } b_1 \vec{v}_1+\cdots+b_n \vec{v}_n = \vec{0} \text{.} \end{equation*}
Therefore by adding these equations:
\begin{equation*} (a_1 + b_1) \vec{v}_1+\cdots+(a_n+b_n) \vec{v}_n = \vec{0}, \end{equation*}
we may conclude that the vector \(\left[\begin{array}{c} a_1+ b_1 \\ \vdots \\ a_n+b_n \end{array}\right] \) is...
  1. another solution.
  2. not a solution.
  3. is equal to \(\vec{0}\text{.}\)
(c)
Similarly, if \(c \in \IR\text{,}\) then since multiplying by \(c\) yields:
\begin{equation*} (ca_1)\vec{v}_1+\cdots+(ca_n)\vec{v}_n=\vec{0}, \end{equation*}
we may conclude that the vector \(\left[\begin{array}{c} ca_1 \\ \vdots \\ ca_n \end{array}\right] \) is...
  1. another solution.
  2. not a solution.
  3. is equal to \(\vec{0}\text{.}\)
  4. The empty set.
