Activity 1.2.2.

Consider whether these matrix manipulations (A) must keep or (B) could change the solution set for the corresponding linear system.
(a)
Swapping two rows, for example:
\begin{equation*} \left[\begin{array}{cc|c} 1 & 2 & 3 \\ 4 & 5 & 6 \end{array}\right] \sim \left[\begin{array}{cc|c} 4 & 5 & 6 \\ 1 & 2 & 3 \end{array}\right] \end{equation*}
\begin{align*} x +2y \amp= 3 \amp 4x + 5y \amp= 6\\ 4x + 5y \amp= 6 \amp x +2y \amp= 3 \end{align*}
(b)
Swapping two columns, for example:
\begin{equation*} \left[\begin{array}{cc|c} 1 & 2 & 3 \\ 4 & 5 & 6 \end{array}\right] \sim \left[\begin{array}{cc|c} 2 & 1 & 3 \\ 5 & 4 & 6 \end{array}\right] \end{equation*}
\begin{align*} x +2y \amp= 3 \amp 2x + y \amp= 3\\ 4x + 5y \amp= 6 \amp 5x +4y \amp= 6 \end{align*}
(c)
Add a constant to every term of a row, for example:
\begin{equation*} \left[\begin{array}{cc|c} 1 & 2 & 3 \\ 4 & 5 & 6 \end{array}\right] \sim \left[\begin{array}{cc|c} 1+6 & 2+6 & 3+6 \\ 4 & 5 & 6 \end{array}\right] \end{equation*}
\begin{align*} x +2y \amp= 3 \amp 7x + 8y \amp= 9\\ 4x + 5y \amp= 6 \amp 4x +5y \amp= 6 \end{align*}
(d)
Multiply a row by a nonzero constant, for example:
\begin{equation*} \left[\begin{array}{cc|c} 1 & 2 & 3 \\ 4 & 5 & 6 \end{array}\right] \sim \left[\begin{array}{cc|c} 3 & 6 & 9 \\ 4 & 5 & 6 \end{array}\right] \end{equation*}
\begin{align*} x +2y \amp= 3 \amp 3x + 6y \amp= 9\\ 4x + 5y \amp= 6 \amp 4x +5y \amp= 6 \end{align*}
(e)
Add a constant multiple of one row to another row, for example:
\begin{equation*} \left[\begin{array}{cc|c} 1 & 2 & 3 \\ 4 & 5 & 6 \end{array}\right] \sim \left[\begin{array}{cc|c} 1 & 2 & 3 \\ 4+3 & 5+6 & 6+9 \end{array}\right] \end{equation*}
\begin{align*} x +2y \amp= 3 \amp \unknown x +\unknown y \amp= \unknown \\ 4x + 5y \amp= 6 \amp \unknown x +\unknown y \amp= \unknown \end{align*}
(f)
Replace a column with zeros, for example:
\begin{equation*} \left[\begin{array}{cc|c} 1 & 2 & 3 \\ 4 & 5 & 6 \end{array}\right] \sim \left[\begin{array}{cc|c} 1 & 0 & 3 \\ 4 & 0 & 6 \end{array}\right] \end{equation*}
\begin{align*} x +2y \amp= 3 \amp \unknown x +\unknown y \amp= \unknown \\ 4x + 5y \amp= 6 \amp \unknown x +\unknown y \amp= \unknown \end{align*}
(g)
Replace a row with zeros, for example:
\begin{equation*} \left[\begin{array}{cc|c} 1 & 2 & 3 \\ 4 & 5 & 6 \end{array}\right] \sim \left[\begin{array}{cc|c} 1 & 2 & 3 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 \end{array}\right] \end{equation*}
\begin{align*} x +2y \amp= 3 \amp \unknown x +\unknown y \amp= \unknown \\ 4x + 5y \amp= 6 \amp \unknown x +\unknown y \amp= \unknown \end{align*}
