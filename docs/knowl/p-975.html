Since \(\Im(T) = \vspan\left\{\left[\begin{array}{c}1 \\ 2 \\ 1 \end{array}\right], \left[\begin{array}{c} 3 \\ 4 \\ 6 \end{array}\right], \left[\begin{array}{c} 2 \\ 6 \\ -1 \end{array}\right], \left[\begin{array}{c} -3 \\ -10 \\ 3 \end{array}\right]\right\}\text{,}\) we simply need to find a linearly independent subset of these four spanning vectors. So we compute

\begin{equation*} \RREF \left[\begin{array}{c}1 & 3 & 2 & -3 \\ 2 & 4 & 6 & -10 \\ 1 & 6 & -1 & 3 \end{array}\right] = \left[\begin{array}{c} 1 & 0 & 5 & -9 \\ 0 & 1 & -1 & 2 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & 0\end{array}\right]. \end{equation*}

Since the first two columns are pivot columns, they form a linearly independent spanning set, so a basis for \(\Im T\) is \(\setList{\left[\begin{array}{c}1\\2\\1 \end{array}\right], \left[\begin{array}{c}3\\4\\6 \end{array}\right]}.\)
