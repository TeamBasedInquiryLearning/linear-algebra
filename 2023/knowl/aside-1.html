We always assume the codomain of our operations is \(V\text{,}\) i.e. that addition is a map \(V \times V \rightarrow V\) and that scalar multiplication is a map \(\IR \times V \rightarrow V\text{.}\)
Likewise, we only consider “real” vector spaces, i.e. those whose scalars come from \(\IR\text{.}\) However, one can similarly define vector spaces with scalars from other fields like the complex or rational numbers.
