If you cannot do any of these, then \(T\) can be proven to be a linear transformation by doing both of the following:
  1. For all \(\vec v,\vec w\in V\) (not just specific values), \(T(\vec v+\vec w)=T(\vec v)+T(\vec w)\text{.}\)
  2. For all \(\vec v\in V\) and \(c\in \IR\) (not just specific values), \(T(c\vec v)=cT(\vec v)\text{.}\)
